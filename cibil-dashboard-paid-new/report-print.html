This section reflects your CIBIL Score, which is widely used by loan providers to evaluate loan applications. Your score ranges between 300 and 900, and is calculated based on the information available in the “Accounts” and “Enquiry” section of your CIBIL Report. The closer your score is to 900, the more confidence the lender will have in your ability to repay the loan. Higher your score, the better chances of your application getting approved.

Please note in some cases you might be displayed a CIBIL Score of “NH” which indicates one of the following 3 things:

  1. You have a credit card or loan account, but no credit activity in the last three years.
  2. Lenders may have made enquiries, but you do not have any credit activity.
  3. You only have add-on credit cards, and no credit exposure.
